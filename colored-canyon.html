About the Colored Canyon

Hidden deep within the Sinai desert mountains near Nuweiba, the Colored Canyon is a spectacularly beautiful labyrinth of sandstone rocks tinged with vibrant shades of yellow, purple, red, and gold. The canyon reaches depths of nearly 40 meters and provides an unforgettable hiking experience.

Formed over millions of years by water erosion from ancient rivers and winter rains, the smooth, colorful, and undulating walls of the canyon make hikers feel as though they are walking through a living geological painting.

Interesting Facts

  • The brilliant colors in the sandstone are produced by copper, iron, cobalt, and magnesium deposits.
  • In some places, the canyon narrows down so much that visitors must squeeze through single file.
  • Unlike many modern trails, this region has been known to the indigenous Bedouin tribes for centuries as a natural shelter.

Sustainability Tips

Responsible Visiting

Stay on marked trails while hiking. Deviating not only damages the fragile desert ecosystem but is also dangerous in remote mountainous areas.

Support Local Businesses

Always hire a local Bedouin guide. The financial support strengthens the local community and they offer unparalleled navigational expertise.

Environmental Notes

Avoid taking "souvenir rocks" from the canyon. The beauty lies in the intact formations. Ensure that absolutely no waste is left behind.
