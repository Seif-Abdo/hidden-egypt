About Siwa Oasis

Located just 50km east of the Libyan border, Siwa Oasis is one of Egypt's most isolated and enchanting settlements. Nestled deep within the Great Sand Sea, Siwa feels like a world apart from the rest of the country, maintaining its own unique culture, language (Siwi), and traditions.

The oasis is famous for its hundreds of natural cold and hot springs, historic fortresses made of traditional mud-brick (Kershef), and sprawling groves of palm and olive trees. From swimming in the pristine salt lakes to hiking the Mountain of the Dead, Siwa offers an incredibly diverse travel experience.

Interesting Facts

  • Alexander the Great famously visited the Oracle of Amun here in 331 BC to confirm his divine origin.
  • The salt pools in Siwa have such a high concentration of salt that you float effortlessly, much like the Dead Sea.
  • The traditional building material, Kershef, is a unique mixture of salt rock from the local lakes and natural mud.

Sustainability Tips

Responsible Visiting

Respect local customs and conservative dress codes in public areas, especially when walking around the town and markets.

Support Local Businesses

Purchase pure olive oil, dates, and local Siwan handicrafts directly from the markets to support the oasis economy.

Environmental Notes

Water is precious in the desert. Even though springs are abundant, practice water conservation when staying at eco-lodges.
